Frequently Asked Questions

How does PurePixel analyze photo quality?
PurePixel uses advanced AI technology to analyze your photos locally on your device. This ensures complete privacy while providing accurate quality assessments for blur detection, duplicate identification, and overall image quality evaluation.
Is my data safe and private?
Absolutely! PurePixel processes all photos locally on your device using on-device AI. No photos or personal data are ever sent to external servers. The app works completely offline, ensuring your privacy is fully protected.
Can I recover deleted photos?
Photos deleted through PurePixel are moved to your device's "Recently Deleted" album, where they remain for 30 days before permanent deletion. You can recover them from there if needed. Always review photos carefully before deletion.
Why is the app scanning slowly?
Scanning speed depends on your device's processing power and the number of photos in your library. The AI analysis is computationally intensive to ensure accuracy. For best performance, ensure your device has sufficient free storage and close other resource-intensive apps.
Does PurePixel work on all iOS devices?
PurePixel currently supports iPhone devices running iOS 15.0 or later. The app is specifically designed and optimized for iPhone with Neural Engine support for best AI performance. iPad support is not available in this version.
How accurate is the duplicate detection?
Our AI-powered duplicate detection uses advanced computer vision algorithms to identify similar images with high accuracy. It can detect exact duplicates, near-duplicates, and similar photos taken in burst mode, while minimizing false positives.

Troubleshooting

App crashes during scanning
This usually occurs due to insufficient memory. Try closing other apps, restarting your device, and ensuring you have at least 1GB of free storage. If the issue persists, try scanning smaller batches of photos.
Permission denied error
Go to Settings > Privacy & Security > Photos, find PurePixel, and ensure "Full Access" is selected. The app requires full photo library access to analyze and manage your photos effectively.
Scanning gets stuck
Force close the app and restart it. If the issue continues, restart your device. Large photo libraries may take considerable time to process - this is normal for the initial scan.
